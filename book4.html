Refugee Synopsis

"Refugee" is a powerful and moving novel by Alan Gratz that tells the story of three interconnected narratives set during the Holocaust and World War II. The book follows the journeys of three young people: Josef, a Jewish boy fleeing Nazi-occupied Czechoslovakia; Isabel, a Cuban girl escaping the political turmoil of her homeland; and Mahmoud, a Syrian boy fleeing the war in his country. Through their experiences, the novel explores themes of survival, resilience, and the universal human desire for safety and belonging. "Refugee" is an emotionally compelling read that highlights the courage and strength of those who have been forced to leave their homes in search of a better life.
